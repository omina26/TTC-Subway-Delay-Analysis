Exploratory Data Analysis

This page shows some interactive plots created for exploratory purposes. Hover over the plots to see more information. You can find a written EDA report here.

Figure A. Delay Map

Figure A. The frequency of TTC subway delays at each station in 2024. Circle size reflects the number of delays, and the popup includes the service population.

According to Figure A, Bloor-Yonge Station had the highest frequency of subway delays across all stations. It also appears that terminal stations had more delays compared to non-terminal stations.

Figure B. Delay vs Service Population

Figure B. Scatterplot of the average TTC Subway delay duration for each subway and line combination by the service population in 2024. Each point shows a station-line combination, with service population in the x-axis and average delay duration in the y-axis.

According to Figure B, there doesnâ€™t appear to be a correlation between service population and average delay duration. On average, average delay duration is between 5 and 10 minutes. The stations with average delay duration greater than 10 minutes have one or two delays. Hence, these were probably caused by an unpredictable emergency situation.

Figure C. Heatmap: Delays by Day and Hour

Figure C. TTC Subway delay frequencys across days of the week and times of the day in 2024. Darker colors indicate more frequent delays during specific hours and days.

According to Figure C, delays are most frequent on SUndays at 8 AM. Further, morning and afternoon rush hours are more prone to delays during the work week.
